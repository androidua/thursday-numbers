📊 How Each Strategy Works

Hot Numbers: Samples all 35 main balls with probability proportional to recency-weighted frequency — balls that have appeared more often in recent draws get a higher chance of selection. Recency is a linear scale: the most recent draw counts twice as much as the oldest, with every draw in between interpolated proportionally.

Cold Numbers: Picks from the 10 least-drawn main balls and least-drawn Powerballs. Based on the gambler's intuition that low-frequency balls are "overdue" — though in a fair random draw, past results never influence future ones.

Balanced Draw: Uses rejection sampling against the theoretical hypergeometric distribution for 7-ball draws from 1–35. Only accepts combinations where the sum falls in the central 90th percentile (87–165), the odd/even split is 2–5 either way, and the low/high split (1–17 vs 18–35) is similarly balanced. This produces a pick that looks statistically "typical" — not biased toward extremes.

True Random: Every number has equal probability, each pick is independent — exactly how the actual draw works. Avoids popular combinations, which reduces jackpot-splitting risk if you ever did win.

⚠️ Important: Powerball is a game of pure chance. Each draw is independent and random. Past results have zero statistical influence on future draws. This tool is for entertainment only.
